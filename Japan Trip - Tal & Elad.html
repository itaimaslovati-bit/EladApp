Based on your route, these are the top things I think you might be missing. Only the highlights — stuff that would be a shame to skip given you're already right there.
Tokyo

Asakusa & Senso-ji Temple

Tokyo's most iconic temple, Nakamise shopping street, and the Kaminarimon gate. You have 4 days in Tokyo at the start but it's not in your plan. Perfect for a half-day, combine with a walk along the Sumida River.

Fits Day 2, 3, or 4
Tokyo

Tsukiji Outer Market

The original fish market moved to Toyosu, but the outer market is still a must-visit for fresh sushi breakfast, tamagoyaki, and street food. 1-2 hours in the morning.

Fits any Tokyo morning (Day 2-4 or 37)
Tokyo (Return)

Akihabara

Electric Town — anime, manga, retro games, maid cafes. Even if you're not into anime, the energy is worth experiencing. Easy half-day.

Fits Day 37 (free Tokyo day)
Kyoto

Fushimi Inari — Decide Yes or No

It's marked with "?" on Day 9. The thousands of torii gates are one of Japan's most photographed sights. Best at sunrise or late afternoon. If doing it, commit to walking at least halfway up the mountain (30-45 min). It's technically in Kyoto, so Day 8 evening or Day 9 morning makes more sense than leaving it to the Osaka travel day.

Move to Day 8 afternoon/evening?
Osaka

Dotonbori & Street Food

Day 11 is a free day but has no plan. Dotonbori is THE Osaka experience — takoyaki, okonomiyaki, glico sign, canal walk. Don't miss it. Combine with Shinsekai for kushikatsu.

Day 11 (free Osaka day)
Osaka

Osaka Castle

Even if you don't go inside, the castle grounds and surrounding park are stunning — especially during cherry blossom season (mid-March could be early bloom). The view from the top floor is great.

Day 11 morning
Central Japan

Takayama Old Town at Night

You're staying in Takayama but your plan jumps straight to the morning market next day. The Sanmachi Suji historic district is atmospheric at night — quiet streets, sake breweries, warm lantern light. Walk it the evening you arrive (Day 12).

Day 12 evening
Okinawa

Taketomi Island Day Trip (from Ishigaki)

Only a 10-minute ferry from Ishigaki. Rent a bicycle and ride around the entire island in 1-2 hours. Traditional Okinawan red-tiled houses, star sand beaches, and water buffalo cart rides. A must-do side trip.

Day 29 or 30 (half-day)
Okinawa

Kabira Bay Glass-Bottom Boat

The most beautiful bay in Ishigaki with turquoise water and coral reefs. Swimming is actually prohibited here, so the glass-bottom boat is the way to see the coral. ~30 min ride.
